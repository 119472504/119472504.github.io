Rugby:

Rugby has been a very big and important aspect of my life from a very young age. I started playing rugby when I
was about 5 years old at my local club, Waterpark R.F.C., a club where my family before me had spent their
years playing at. The club has produced excellent teams throughout the years that have gone on to win provincial and
natioal titles such as the PAN Munster U16, U18 and the All-Ireland U18 Cup as well as dominating the East Munster Cup
throughout the past few years. I loved every second of my time at Waterpark and remember fondly all the memories I have
of playing in the Black and Red Jersey.

Hurling:

Another of my main sporting interests would have to be hurling. The GAA has a big influence in my life. I play for Ballgunner, my local club,
and through it I've made lifelong friends. I also use hurling not only as a form of excercise but also as an outlet or break away from all the
other things in my life whether that is from college work or part time work. It acts as a stress reliever and even anytime that I may
have worries all I have to do is go outside and pick up the hurley and it does the world of good.

Gaelic Football:

I've always been passionate about playing football but out of all my sports it's the one where I can relax the most and just enjoy playing
because growing up in Waterford football wasn't exactly a competitive sport. It's mostly played in the summer for a bit of a laugh but that's
one of the reasons I enjoy it so much. I don't have to take it as seriously as my rugby or hurling as there isn't a lot of competitive
tournaments. It's mostly just played as challenge matches between the differnt parishes. It's always good to play a sport where there isn't
the added pressure of trying to win a cup or a league and that's why I've love my time playing for Gailltir.
